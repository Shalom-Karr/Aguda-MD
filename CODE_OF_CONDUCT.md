# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

This project exists to help vulnerable Maryland residents access government assistance. Everyone who participates in the project — by contributing code, filing issues, reviewing pull requests, or interacting in any other project space — must conduct themselves in a way that keeps the project welcoming, respectful, and focused on that mission.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful. Disagree on the substance, not the person.
- Assume good intent. If a comment can be read two ways, read it the better way and ask for clarification before reacting.
- Use clear, plain-language English. The project serves a multilingual community; don't gatekeep with jargon.
- Credit others. Acknowledge ideas, code, and reviews you build on.
- Keep discussions on-topic and in the appropriate channel (issue, PR, email).

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments — including those framed as jokes.
- Harassment of any kind, public or private.
- Discrimination on the basis of race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, immigration status, or socioeconomic background.
- Sharing private information about another participant without consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or releases.
- Posting sexual or violent imagery, or sexually explicit comments.

The fact that the unacceptable behavior was directed at a contributor's *ideas* rather than *the contributor* does not make it acceptable. Hostility toward newcomers, dismissive review comments, and "this is how I am, deal with it" responses are not welcome.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — the GitHub repository, issue and PR comments, project email, and any in-person or video meetings related to the project. It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces (for example, posting from an account clearly identified with the project).

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it privately to the project maintainer:

- **Shalom Karr** — via the contact form at <https://shalomkarr.pages.dev>

Reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All reports will be handled with discretion. The maintainer is committed to protecting the privacy of the reporter.

In your report, please include:

- Your contact information.
- Names (real or usernames) of any individuals involved.
- A description of the incident, including links if applicable.
- The date and time, if relevant.
- Any additional context that would help us understand the situation.

## Enforcement

The maintainer is responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Possible responses, depending on severity, include:

1. **Private warning** — a private conversation explaining the violation and why the behavior was inappropriate.
2. **Public correction** — a public comment in the venue where the violation occurred.
3. **Temporary ban** — temporary removal from project spaces for a specified period.
4. **Permanent ban** — permanent removal from all project spaces.

The maintainer has the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.
